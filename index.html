What is EV?


An electric vehicle (EV) is one that operates on an electric motor, instead of an internal-combustion engine (ICEs) that generates power by burning a mix of fuel and gases. Therefore, such as vehicle is seen as a possible replacement for current-generation automobile, in order to address the issue of rising pollution, global warming, depleting natural resources, etc.

BEV

Battery Electric Vehicles

Fully-electric vehicles with rechargeable batteries

Battery power is used to run the electric motor and all onboard electronics

Tesla Model 3/S/X
BMW i3
Chevy Bolt
Nissan LEAF
Hyundai Ioniq

PHEV

Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles

PHEVs can recharge the battery through both regenerative braking and “plugging in” to an external source of electrical power

Can go anywhere from 10-40 miles before their gas engines provide assistance

Ford Fusion Energi
Chrysler Pacifica
Chevy Volt

HEV

Hybrid Electric Vehicles

HEVs are powered by both gasoline and electricity

The electric energy is generated by the car’s own "Regenerative braking system" to recharge the battery

Toyota Prius Hybrid
Honda Civic Hybrid
Toyota Camry Hybrid
Toyota Highlander Hybrid

NEV or ZEV

A ZEV is a vehicle that has the potential to produce no tailpipe emissions
They can still have a conventional internal combustion engine, but must also be able to operate without using it

Example, BEV, PHEV or Hydrogen Fuel cell

NEV is the term used in China for ZEV, to designate EVs eligible for public subsidies
